Wraith automatically discovers your services from traces, logs, and alerts — then maps how they connect.

## Service graph

An interactive force-directed graph showing every service and how they depend on each other.

**Each node shows:**
- Service name and health status (up, degraded, down, unknown)
- Environment and team assignment
- Error rate and average latency
- Recent trace and error counts
- Confidence score (how certain Wraith is about this service)

**Each edge shows:**
- Dependency type (service, database, cache, queue, external)
- Confidence score
- Cross-verification status (found in traces, logs, or both)

<Info>
  Services found in **both** traces and logs have the highest confidence. Services found in only one source may need additional instrumentation.
</Info>

## How services are discovered

Wraith discovers services from multiple sources:

| Source | What it finds |
|--------|--------------|
| **Traces** | Services instrumented with the SDK |
| **Logs** | Services referenced in CloudWatch or Datadog logs |
| **Alerts** | Services named in monitoring tool alerts |
| **Log groups** | AWS log group naming conventions (`/aws/lambda/service-name`, `/ecs/service-name`) |

## Service health

Health is updated automatically based on:

- **Error logs** — error-level logs mark a service as degraded or down
- **Trace failures** — failed verifications affect service health
- **Alert frequency** — high alert frequency lowers health score
- **No recent data** — services without recent traces or logs are marked unknown

## Dependency types

| Type | Icon color | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Service** | Blue | Another microservice |
| **Database** | Green | PostgreSQL, MySQL, etc. |
| **Cache** | Yellow | Redis, Memcached |
| **Queue** | Orange | SQS, RabbitMQ, Kafka |
| **External** | Gray | Third-party APIs |

## Using the graph

- **Zoom and pan** to explore your topology
- **Click a service** to see its details, endpoints, and recent incidents
- **Filter by name** to find a specific service
- **Color coding** shows health at a glance — green is healthy, red is down

<Tip>
  Use the service graph during an incident to understand blast radius. If a core service is down, the graph shows which downstream services are affected.
</Tip>
